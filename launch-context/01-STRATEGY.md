# Launchframe Practice — Launch Strategy

Source context:

- `00-org-context.json`
- `00-brand-context.md`

## Product In One Breath

Launchframe is an OSS launch-control workspace for developer-tool launches. It turns positioning, HN/Reddit plans, surface reservations, brand assets, repo readiness, and launch-day execution into one auditable release-style system.

## Launch Goal

Practice and validate the reusable launch process before applying it to a real OSS product.

This run uses low-risk real rehearsal surfaces so the whole process can be rehearsed without public posting.

## Audience

1. Primary: founders launching OSS developer tools.
2. Secondary: devrel leads and small engineering teams.
3. Tertiary: agents/operators coordinating launch collateral across repos, sites, assets, and channels.

## Category Attachment

Launchframe should attach to release engineering and launch readiness, not generic marketing planning.

Avoid being mistaken for:

- A generic Notion launch checklist.
- A project-management app.
- A growth-hacking tool.
- An AI copy generator.
- A services agency process.

## Competitive Wedge

| Alternative | What it does | Why this is different |
|---|---|---|
| Notion/Docs | Drafts and checklists | Launchframe ties launch docs to proof surfaces, public readiness gates, templates, and review artifacts. |
| Linear/Jira/GitHub Issues | Tracks work | Launchframe tracks launch-specific claims, surfaces, collateral, and launch-day execution. |
| Agency launch plans | Expert execution | Launchframe makes the launch process repeatable inside the repo. |
| AI chat transcripts | Generates research/copy/assets | Launchframe preserves context, decisions, prompts, images, reviews, and blockers outside ephemeral transcripts. |

## Core Demo

The practice demo is this workspace:

1. `00-org-context.json` captures structured product/org/surface facts.
2. `00-brand-context.md` captures positioning, voice, visual direction, claims, and objections.
3. `00-MASTER-CHECKLIST.md` turns the context into explicit launch gates.
4. `../../templates/repo/` and `../../templates/policies/` prevent agents from freehanding critical OSS/policy docs.
5. `assets/`, `site/`, and `review/` show the Gemini/Nano Banana visual workflow and readiness review.

Expected result: dress rehearsal readiness passes, while real external publication remains a separate approval.

## Why Now

AI-assisted launch work can produce a lot of useful material quickly, but it also loses context, over-generates copy, invents external surfaces, and blurs verified product truth. OSS launches need a repeatable system that makes every claim, asset, policy, and channel artifact traceable.

## Proof Points

- Reconstructed ztrack launch process exists in `../../playbook/`.
- Launchframe practice visual workflow exists in `assets/`, `brand/`, `site/`, and `review/`.
- Gemini Cycle 3 review passed practice-site readiness and separated production gaps.
- Repo and policy templates exist in `../../templates/repo/` and `../../templates/policies/`.
- The master checklist now starts from cross-cutting context instead of scattered prompts.

## Traps To Avoid

- Claiming Launchframe is a packaged app before package/repo/install exists.
- Making the site the launch instead of one gate in the broader process.
- Letting agents write privacy, terms, security, conduct, or license language from scratch.
- Inventing GitHub, Discord, npm, domain, or support surfaces.
- Turning the brand into generic SaaS or purple AI-tool language.
- Treating a passed practice site review as production launch readiness.

## Narrative Arc

1. OSS launch work starts scattered across docs, chats, screenshots, and half-verified claims.
2. The launch should be treated like release engineering.
3. Launchframe centralizes context, surfaces, templates, collateral, assets, reviews, and runbooks.
4. Every public claim maps to a proof surface, a rehearsal surface, or an explicitly deferred production target.
5. The practice workspace proves the process; production launch requires real public surfaces.
